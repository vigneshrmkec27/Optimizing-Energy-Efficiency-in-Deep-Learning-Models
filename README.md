# Green AI: Optimizing Energy Efficiency in Deep Learning Models

## Overview
This repository contains the research paper **"Green AI: Optimizing Energy Efficiency in Deep Learning Models."** The study explores techniques to enhance energy efficiency in Artificial Intelligence (AI) models using pruning, quantization, and efficient architectures[cite: 1, 14].

With the growing adoption of AI in mobile devices, IoT systems, and data centers, the need for energy-efficient AI models has become critical. Traditional deep learning models require high computational power, leading to increased energy usage. This paper proposes a hybrid optimization strategy that integrates pruning and quantization to make AI inference faster and more power-efficient, achieving up to 50% energy savings with minimal accuracy loss[cite: 1, 14].

## Key Optimization Strategies
* **Adaptive Pruning:** Dynamically removes redundant connections in the neural network based on importance[cite: 1, 14].
* **Quantization-Aware Training:** Reduces computation precision to 8-bit or lower while maintaining accuracy[cite: 1, 14].
* **Hardware-Aware AI:** Explores the impact of hardware architectures (CPU, GPU, TPU) on energy efficiency, noting that TPUs consume 40% less power than GPUs while maintaining speed[cite: 1, 14].

## Tools & Frameworks Evaluated
* **TensorFlow & Model Optimization Toolkit:** For implementing pruning and quantization techniques[cite: 1, 14].
* **MATLAB:** For power consumption simulations[cite: 1, 14].
* **Python (Matplotlib):** For visualizing improvements and generating plots[cite: 1, 14].
